1. Cell Structure and Functions

The cell is the basic structural and functional unit of life. Human cells contain organelles such as nucleus, mitochondria, endoplasmic reticulum, Golgi apparatus, and lysosomes, each performing specific functions.

2. Tissues of the Human Body

A tissue is a group of similar cells performing a common function. The four basic tissues are:

  • Epithelial tissue
  • Connective tissue
  • Muscle tissue
  • Nervous tissue

3. Epithelial Tissue

Epithelial tissue covers body surfaces, lines cavities, and forms glands. It functions in protection, absorption, secretion, and filtration.

4. Connective Tissue

Connective tissue supports, binds, and protects organs. Examples include bone, cartilage, blood, and adipose tissue.
