1. Anatomy and Physiology

Anatomy is the branch of science that deals with the structure of the human body, whereas physiology deals with the functions of different organs and systems. Both anatomy and physiology are closely related and essential for understanding the normal functioning of the human body.

2. Levels of Organization

The human body is organized into several structural levels:

  • Chemical level (atoms and molecules)
  • Cellular level
  • Tissue level
  • Organ level
  • System level
  • Organism level

3. Basic Life Processes

Basic life processes include metabolism, responsiveness, movement, growth, differentiation, and reproduction. These processes are essential for maintaining life and homeostasis.

4. Homeostasis

Homeostasis refers to the maintenance of a relatively stable internal environment within the body. It is regulated by feedback mechanisms, primarily negative feedback systems.

5. Terminology and Anatomical Position

Anatomical position is a standard reference position used to describe the location of body parts. Anatomical terms such as anterior, posterior, medial, lateral, proximal, and distal are used to describe body relationships accurately.
