1. Introduction to Calculus

Calculus is the branch of mathematics that deals with rates of change and accumulation. It includes differentiation and integration, which are widely used in scientific calculations.

2. Differentiation

Differentiation is the process of finding the rate of change of one quantity with respect to another. It is used to determine slopes, maxima and minima, and rates.

3. Rules of Differentiation

  • Power rule
  • Constant rule
  • Sum and difference rule
  • Product rule
  • Quotient rule

4. Integration

Integration is the reverse process of differentiation. It is used to calculate areas, volumes, and total quantities from rates of change.

5. Applications of Calculus in Pharmacy

  • Pharmacokinetic calculations
  • Drug release rate studies
  • Data analysis and modeling
  • Determination of maxima and minima
