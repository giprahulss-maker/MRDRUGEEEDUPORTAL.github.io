1. Introduction to Environmental Sciences

Environmental Sciences is an interdisciplinary field that studies the interaction between human beings and the natural environment. It focuses on understanding environmental problems and finding sustainable solutions.

2. Environment

Environment includes all living and non-living components surrounding organisms. It consists of physical, chemical, biological, and social factors that influence life.

3. Components of Environment

  • Atmosphere ‚Äì layer of gases surrounding the Earth
  • Hydrosphere ‚Äì water bodies like oceans, rivers, and lakes
  • Lithosphere ‚Äì land and soil
  • Biosphere ‚Äì living organisms

4. Scope and Importance of Environmental Studies

Environmental studies help in understanding natural resources, environmental pollution, conservation strategies, and sustainable development.

5. Multidisciplinary Nature of Environmental Studies

Environmental studies involve various disciplines such as biology, chemistry, geology, sociology, economics, and pharmacy.

6. Sustainability and Sustainable Development

Sustainable development refers to development that meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.

7. Role of Environmental Sciences in Pharmacy

  • Safe disposal of pharmaceutical waste
  • Environmental impact of drug manufacturing
  • Prevention of pollution caused by pharmaceuticals
