1. Pharmaceutical Suspensions

Suspensions are heterogeneous liquid dosage forms in which insoluble solid particles are dispersed uniformly throughout a liquid medium. They are commonly used when drugs are insoluble or unstable in solution.

2. Advantages and Disadvantages of Suspensions

  • Better stability than solutions for certain drugs
  • Masking of unpleasant taste
  • Accurate dosing possible after shaking
  • Require shaking before use

3. Ideal Characteristics of a Pharmaceutical Suspension

  • Uniform particle size distribution
  • Slow rate of sedimentation
  • Easy redispersibility
  • Pleasant appearance and palatability

4. Formulation of Suspensions

Suspensions are formulated using suspending agents, wetting agents, preservatives, sweeteners, and flavoring agents to ensure stability and patient acceptability.

5. Applications of Suspensions

Pharmaceutical suspensions are widely used for oral, topical, and parenteral administration of drugs that are poorly soluble in water.
