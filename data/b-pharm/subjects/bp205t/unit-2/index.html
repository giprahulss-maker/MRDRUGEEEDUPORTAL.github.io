1. Introduction to MS Office

Microsoft Office is a collection of productivity software used for documentation, data management, presentations, and communication. It is widely used in academic institutions and pharmacy practice.

2. MS Word

MS Word is a word processing application used to create, edit, and format documents such as reports, assignments, prescriptions, and research manuscripts.

  • Text formatting and styles
  • Tables, images, and charts
  • Headers, footers, and page numbering

3. MS Excel

MS Excel is a spreadsheet application used for data analysis, calculations, and graphical representation of data.

  • Formulas and functions
  • Charts and graphs
  • Data sorting and filtering

4. MS PowerPoint

MS PowerPoint is used to create presentations for lectures, seminars, and academic conferences.

  • Slide creation and design
  • Animations and transitions
  • Multimedia integration

5. Introduction to Database

A database is an organized collection of data that can be easily accessed, managed, and updated. Databases are essential in pharmacy for storing patient records and drug information.

6. Database Management System (DBMS)

DBMS is software that enables users to define, create, maintain, and control access to databases.

7. Applications of Databases in Pharmacy

  • Patient medication records
  • Inventory and stock management
  • Billing and prescription tracking
  • Drug information systems
